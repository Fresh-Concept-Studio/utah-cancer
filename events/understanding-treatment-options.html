About this event

Whether you're newly diagnosed or supporting someone who is, the language of cancer treatment can feel overwhelming. This 90-minute session breaks it down.

Led by one of our medical oncologists alongside a patient navigator, we'll walk through the most common treatment modalities — surgery, radiation, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy — and what each involves. We'll cover how decisions are made, what questions to ask your care team, and how to navigate second opinions. The session is interactive: bring your questions.

What to expect

  • Plain-language overview of the major treatment types and when they're used.
  • How treatment plans are built and the role of multidisciplinary tumor boards.
  • Questions to ask before, during, and after each appointment.
  • Navigating second opinions without offending your care team.
  • Open Q&A the last 30 minutes are reserved for live questions.

Frequently asked

Do I need to register in advance?

Yes — registration is free, but it's required so we can send you the Zoom link.

Will it be recorded?

Yes. Registered attendees receive a link to the recording within 48 hours.

Can I stay anonymous in the Q&A?

Absolutely. You can submit questions through the chat without using your name.

Is this medical advice?

No. The session is educational. For specific guidance about your situation, please bring questions to your care team.
