Common questions

What patients ask most.

If you don’t see your question here, our research team is happy to answer it directly — reach out to either program above.

What is a clinical trial?

A clinical trial is a carefully designed research study that tests new treatments in patients. All trials are reviewed and approved by an Institutional Review Board (IRB) to ensure patient safety, and participation is always voluntary.

Will I receive a placebo?

Most cancer clinical trials do not use placebos alone. When a control arm is included, participants always receive at least the current standard of care in addition to any investigational agent.

Are there costs to participate?

Investigational drugs and study-related tests are typically provided at no cost. Standard-of-care costs (like routine blood work) are generally billed to your insurance as they normally would be.

How do I find out if I’m eligible?

Ask your oncologist about trial options at your next visit, or contact our research team directly. We’ll review your diagnosis, treatment history, and molecular profile to identify suitable trials.

Can I leave a trial if I change my mind?

Yes. Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw from a clinical trial at any time, for any reason, without affecting your ongoing care at Utah Cancer Specialists.
