About this event

Cancer changes the shape of a life, and the months after treatment can feel quieter and stranger than expected. This group exists to make that quiet less lonely.

Facilitated by a licensed oncology social worker, our survivorship group meets the last Thursday of every month. There's no curriculum — just conversation, coffee, and a confidential space to share what you're navigating. New faces and longtime members are equally welcome. Family members and primary caregivers are welcome too.

What to expect

  • Confidentiality what's said in the room stays in the room.
  • A facilitator, not a teacher our oncology social worker keeps things gentle, not clinical.
  • Low pressure share as much or as little as feels right.
  • Coffee & tea free, always.
  • A real community many members have been coming for years.

Frequently asked

Do I need to RSVP?

No, but it helps us know how much coffee to make. RSVPs aren't required.

Is this only for current patients?

No — survivors at any stage are welcome, including those who finished treatment years ago.

Can my partner come?

Yes. Caregivers and close family members are welcome too.

What kinds of cancers does the group cover?

All types. The conversations are about the experience of survivorship, not specific treatments.
