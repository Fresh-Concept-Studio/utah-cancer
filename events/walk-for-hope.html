About this event

For nearly two decades, the Walk for Hope has been our largest community gathering of the year — a morning of movement, memory, and momentum.

Patients, survivors, families, providers, and friends meet at the gates of Sugar House Park for a 5K route through one of Salt Lake's most beautiful green spaces. Every step funds patient assistance programs, lodging stipends for traveling patients, and clinical trial scholarships. Walk fast, walk slow, walk in honor of someone — or just come for the breakfast burritos and live music. There's a place for everyone.

What to expect

  • Family-friendly 5K route through Sugar House Park — strollers, dogs on leashes, and walkers welcome.
  • Survivor recognition at the start line, with a short ceremony before the walk begins.
  • Breakfast & coffee from local sponsors at the finish line.
  • Live acoustic music at the pavilion from 9:00 AM onward.
  • Kids' tent with face painting, crafts, and a photo booth.
  • Provider meet-and-greet at the UCS info booth from 10:00 AM – noon.

Frequently asked

Do I need to register in advance?

Day-of registration is welcome, but pre-registration guarantees a t-shirt in your size and a smoother check-in. Online registration closes at midnight on June 13.

Is the route accessible?

Yes. The 5K route is paved the entire way, with gentle elevation changes. Wheelchairs, walkers, strollers, and assistive devices are all welcome. A 1-mile shorter route is also available for anyone who prefers.

What if it rains?

The walk happens rain or shine. Heavy lightning would shift the start time but not cancel the event — we'll communicate updates by email and on our social channels.

Can I walk in honor or in memory of someone?

Absolutely. The "I walk for..." badge is included in every check-in packet. Honoree names are also added to our community ribbon wall at the finish line.

Are dogs allowed?

Friendly, leashed dogs are welcome on the 5K route. Please bring waste bags and water for your pup.
