About this event

Caregiving is one of the most quietly demanding roles a person can take on. This morning is for you.

Led by our oncology nurse navigators and a patient and family services counselor, this workshop covers the practical and emotional sides of caregiving. We'll talk about logistics — medications, appointments, paperwork — and equally about boundaries, burnout, and how to ask for help. Bring your questions. Come early for coffee.

What to expect

  • Practical caregiving toolkit medication tracking, appointment prep, communicating with the care team.
  • Talking to kids age-appropriate language for explaining a parent's or grandparent's diagnosis.
  • Boundaries & burnout how to spot caregiver fatigue and what to do about it.
  • Resources we wish more people knew about respite services, financial counseling, support groups.
  • Time to talk small-group breakouts in the second hour.

Frequently asked

Is this only for spouses?

No. Adult children, siblings, friends, neighbors — anyone in a caregiving role is welcome.

Should the patient come too?

It's designed for caregivers, but you're welcome to bring your loved one if it helps.

Will I have to share?

Sharing is always optional. Many people come and just listen.

Is this faith-based?

No. The workshop is secular and welcoming of every background.
